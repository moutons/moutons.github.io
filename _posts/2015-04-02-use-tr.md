### use tr

That's it, really. I needed a reason to post, and I need to remind myself to use tr more often. I find myself trying to go out of my way to remember how to force sed to be aware of newlines, but the truth of the matter is that it's better to use tr since a simple `cat $filename | tr "\n" " "` does exactly what I probably want. Likely, `cat $filename` isn't necessary, just pipe `sed` to `tr` or something.
